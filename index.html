GI Tract Image Segmentation (Kaggle Competition 2022)

In 2019, around 5 million people globally were diagnosed with gastrointestinal cancers, with approximately half requiring radiation therapy. Advanced technology, such as MR-Linacs, enables oncologists to visualize these shifts, but requires the time-consuming process of manually outlining the stomach and intestines in MRI scans to adjust radiation beams accurately. Automating this segmentation process using deep learning could significantly reduce treatment times, allowing more patients to receive precise and effective care. This project, supported by the UW-Madison Carbone Cancer Center, aims to develop models to automatically segment these organs on MRI scans, enhancing the efficiency and accuracy of radiation therapy.

Brain Tumor Classification Image Segmentation (Kaggle)

Brain Tumors are growth of cells in the brain or around it.These tumors can occur in the brain tissue itself or near the brain in areas such as the nerves , pituitary glands among others. There are over 120 brain tumor types, based on the brain tissues they affect. Not all brain tumors are brain cancer, but even benign (or noncancerous) tumors can be dangerous because of their size or location.In this project I classified CT scans of the Brain under 4 different Categories; no_tumor, glioma_tumor, pituitary_tumor, meningioma_tumor.

Zindi Africa Urban Air Pollution Challenge (2020)

Recent reports have suggested that air quality has improved in some areas due to the impact of COVID-19. While this is true for certain locations, the situation is more complex. In several African cities, for instance, air quality may be deteriorating as more people remain indoors. In this challenge, we aim to delve deeper into the data to explore how air quality is changing, even in regions lacking ground-based sensors.
